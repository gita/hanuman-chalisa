Hanuman Chalisa is a religious hymn(strotra) in Awadhi language in praise of Lord Hanuman.

Hanuman Chalisa is a religious hymn(strotra) composed by Goswami Tulsidas in Awadhi language in praise of Lord Hanuman.

The word Chalisa means 40 verses and is derived from the word Chalis which means 40 in Sanskrit. Hanuman Chalisa is a devotional hymn of 40 verses in praise of Lord Hanuman.

Tulsidas' Hanuman Chalisa describes the empathy and support of Hanuman to those who seek his help or pray to Lord Rama. Tulsidas has written this to inspire spiritual aspirants to become devotees of Lord Rama so that they are blessed and protected by Hanuman and are spiritually progressing.

By reciting Hanuman Chalisa, a devotee reminds himself of the wisdom, strength, courage, celibacy of Hanuman, his devotion towards Lord Ram and his fights against unrighteous. Hanuman is the source of immense energy and focus. Reciting Hanuman Chalisa channelizes energy and gives a total focus of mind and body.
